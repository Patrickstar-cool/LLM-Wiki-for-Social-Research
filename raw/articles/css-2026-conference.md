# CSS 2026 Santa Fe

## The Computational Social Science Society of the Americas

Join us at CSS 2026, the premier annual gathering for computational social scientists, to explore cutting-edge research and foster interdisciplinary collaboration. The conference will be held in person at The Drury Plaza Hotel in Santa Fe, New Mexico, October 29 – November 1, 2026.

The CSSSA defines computational social science broadly as the theory-driven application of computational methods to analyze, model, and simulate social systems. This includes analyzing large-scale social data, exploring mechanisms underlying social dynamics through simulation, and connecting insights to social science theory.

We especially encourage submissions that address applied computational social science to demonstrate real-world impact. Advances in artificial intelligence and machine learning are opening new avenues for CSS research, and we welcome work that explores these methods alongside the full range of computational approaches that define the field.

**Keynote Speakers**: Ada Palmer & Marco Janssen

**Topics of Interest:**
- Modeling & Simulation: Agent-Based Modeling, Tools and Frameworks, Validation Techniques, AI-Assisted Model Generation
- Data & Social Dynamics: Social Network Analysis, Cultural Change, Economic Modeling, Machine Learning for Social Data, NLP and LLMs in CSS Research
- Applied CSS in Policy & Governance: Public Health, Disease Spread, Policy Analysis, Institutional Design
- Foundations & Ethics: Theoretical Foundations, Ethical Considerations, Social Impacts of AI and Algorithmic Systems
- Real-World Applications & Case Studies

**Important Dates:**
- Submission Deadline: July 15, 2026 (extended from June 30)
- Author Notification: August 1, 2026
- Conference: October 29–November 1, 2026
