Anning Hu (2024). "The Differential Mode of Association in Contemporary China: An Enriched Portrayal." The China Review, Vol. 24, No. 1 (February 2024), 233–250.

Anning Hu is Professor of Sociology at Fudan University. Supported by the National Social Science Foundation Project (22VRC140).

## Abstract

Drawing on the 2022 Traditional Culture and Cognitive Pattern Survey (TCCPS), this article reports an enriched portrayal of chaxugeju (the differential mode of association) in today's China, an influential sociological conception coined by Fei Xiaotong.

## Key Empirical Findings

1. **Differential mode persists**: The interpersonal connections of Chinese citizens still manifest a typical differential mode of association from family members (such as parents) through to ordinary acquaintances. This differential mode is more salient among rural residents.

2. **Kinship vs. non-kinship distinction**: The kinship-versus-non-kinship distinction stands out with significantly higher trust in kinship members.

3. **Within kinship**: Senior and younger generations are perceived to be more trustworthy than referents of the contemporaneous generation.

4. **Conjugal relationship**: The conjugal relationship matters, as reflected by the strong trust in the spouse.

5. **Sibling effect**: Partly due to the one-child policy, trust in brothers and sisters declines with age.

6. **Patrilineal principle NOT supported**: Kinship members of both maternal and paternal descent lines are seen to be comparably trustworthy. This challenges a traditional assumption about Chinese kinship.

7. **Non-kinship domain is tripartite**: The most trusted close friends, the intermediately trusted people with socially meaningful ties, and the least trusted strangers.

## Theoretical Significance

This is one of the first large-scale empirical investigations of chaxugeju in contemporary China, providing both confirmation of the concept's continued relevance and nuanced modifications (e.g., the decline of patrilineal principle, the rise of conjugal bonds).
