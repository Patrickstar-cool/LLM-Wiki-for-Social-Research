# Is social theory losing its relevance?

Published in The Journal of Chinese Sociology, June 2025. Open Access (CC BY 4.0).

## Abstract

Currently, the interest in and use of social theory by policy-makers, organizations, and citizens have decreased worldwide. While excellent research focuses on the social influences of social theory and teaching methods, the research often neglects the link between social theory and contemporary societal needs. This gap is addressed by examining the social impact and effects on the motivation for using dialogic theory, which emphasizes dialog and consensus building. 

Key findings:
- Habermas citations dropped from 129 (2023) to 121 (2024)
- Foucault citations dropped from 37 to 25
- Giddens citations dropped from 33 to 22
- The paper advocates for "dialogic sociology" that bridges theory and practice
- References Chinese social theory development and China's 2024 proposal for an independent knowledge system in philosophy and social science

The study includes nine communicative interviews and three participatory observations with readers of *The Dialogic Society* by Flecha. Findings reveal: increased motivation for sociology, deeper comprehension, potential for transforming social contexts, and a shift toward cocreation and social impact.
