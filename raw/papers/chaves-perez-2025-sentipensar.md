# Decolonial knowledge in Practice: a mestiza reflection on sentipensar in indigenous Nasa epistemologies

**Authors:** Paola Chaves Pérez (Radboud University Nijmegen)
**Published:** Frontiers in Sociology, 25 September 2025
**DOI:** 10.3389/fsoc.2025.1605618
**Citations:** 3

## Core Argument

This paper presents the concept of *sentipensar* (feeling-thinking) as practiced by the Nasa indigenous people of Colombia. *Sentipensar* was coined by Colombian sociologist Orlando Fals Borda in the 1970s and refers to the inseparability of reason, emotion, body, spirit, and land. The paper argues that *sentipensar* is not merely an alternative epistemology but a **decolonial epistemology in its own right** — one that challenges the Cartesian dualisms foundational to Western science.

## Key Concepts

- **Sentipensar**: Thinking with the body/heart; feeling-thinking simultaneously; erases the divide between rationality and emotion
- **Mestiza consciousness** (Anzaldúa): Tolerance for contradiction and ambiguity
- **Ch'ixi epistemology** (Rivera Cusicanqui): Coexistence of multiple, contradictory ways of knowing without synthesis
- **Controlled equivocation** (Viveiros de Castro): Recognizing asymmetries in translating Indigenous concepts for Western audiences
- **Body as first territory**: Nasa understanding that knowledge is produced through the body, not abstract reasoning

## Nasa Practices Documented

1. **Walking the territory** (*kiwe*): Embodied knowledge through patrolling, observing; territory is alive and communicates
2. **Rituals** (*refrescamientos*, *limpiezas*): Protection and cleansing through coca, alcohol, medicinal herbs — not explained but learned through attentive observation
3. **Señas**: Bodily signals (itches, vibrations) that communicate messages; interpreted by *Thë'Walas* (shamans)
4. **Tulpa**: Three-stone fire as epistemic site — space for intergenerational knowledge transmission and collective deliberation
5. **Guardia Indígena**: Unarmed community protection network using collective *sentipensar* and horizontal organization

## Methodological Contribution

The author used Participatory Action Research (PAR) — developed by Fals Borda in response to decontextualized knowledge production. PAR emphasizes reciprocity, co-learning, and ethical commitment to community-defined goals. The author argues that *sentipensar* became her epistemological approach during fieldwork.

## Significance

*Sentipensar* offers a liberatory epistemic tool grounded in traditions of resistance, healing, and survival under colonial conditions. It challenges the epistemic injustice of dominant conceptual frameworks that cannot even recognize the full meaning of other ways of knowing.
