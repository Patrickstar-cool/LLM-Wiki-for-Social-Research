# Pathways for pragmatic decolonisation in research

**Author:** Monique Kwachou (University of Bristol)
**Published:** Open Research Europe, 17 April 2025
**DOI:** 10.12688/openreseurope.19943.1
**PMID:** 40496704

## Core Argument

This paper argues that **decentralisation** — a concept familiar in governance — offers a useful metaphor and framework for rethinking how power over knowledge production can be redistributed. Just as decentralisation shifts decision-making from central authorities to local communities, decolonising research involves redistributing power over knowledge production to African scholars, institutions, and communities.

## Three Pathways

1. **Theoretical pathways**: Reclaim African epistemologies as valid and generative, disrupting Eurocentric dominance
2. **Methodological pathways**: Advance participatory, Afrocentric approaches grounded in lived experience and relational ethics
3. **Administrative pathways**: Institutional reforms empowering African scholars in shaping research agendas, resource flows, and dissemination

## Key Critique

The paper warns against conceptual dilution of "decolonisation" — citing Tuck & Yang (2012) who argue decolonisation is often treated as a metaphor for social justice rather than a concrete, material process addressing land dispossession and settler sovereignty (drawing on Fanon).

## Epistemic Decentralisation Framework

- **Starting point**: Recognize that Western knowledge dominance is a legacy of colonization, not inherent superiority
- **Theory**: Center African concepts and philosophies (Ubuntu, etc.) as primary analytical frameworks
- **Method**: Use participatory, community-grounded approaches; reject extractive research models
- **Administration**: Reform funding, publishing, and curriculum to empower African institutions

## Significance

Provides an accessible and actionable model for transforming knowledge production, bridging the theory-practice gap in decolonial discourse. Grounded in the author's experiences in Cameroonian higher education.
